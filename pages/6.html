My boots were blue,
with yellow sunflowers.

They were for stomping.
In puddles, down the sidewalk, through the dirt in the garden.

They were for exploring and finding rocks and leaves and twigs
for making little houses in the tree trunks.

My boots took me everywhere, the sunflowers took me everywhere.

Now sunflowers remind me of the places I've traveled, imaginary and real.
They remind me of being outside for too long, of sweaty hands, and childhood.

Sunflowers remind me of me when I was small, she inspires me.
I like how big they felt then and how they still feel big now.
I am glad that they are everywhere.
